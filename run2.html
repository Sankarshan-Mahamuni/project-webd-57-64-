Detective Quiz

Question 1:

The body of Mr. Johnson, a renowned art collector, was discovered in his mansion's library. The room was locked from the inside, and there were no signs of forced entry. Investigating officers found three items near the body: a torn painting, a half-empty glass of wine, and a peculiar footprint on the carpet. Additionally, there was a distinct smell of almonds in the air. Who might be the prime suspect, and what could be the motive?

Clues: The torn painting suggests a struggle or altercation occurred in the room.
The half-empty glass of wine hints at a possible social gathering or rendezvous.
The peculiar footprint on the carpet might indicate the presence of an intruder.
The distinct smell of almonds raises suspicions of cyanide poisoning.

  • A) Mrs. Johnson - Motive: Inheritance
  • B) Mr. Johnson's business partner - Motive: Financial dispute
  • C) The butler - Motive: Personal vendetta
  • D) A random burglar - Motive: Theft
